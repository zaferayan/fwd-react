## 📚 Books

These are the books I keep coming back to. Some I've read cover to cover, some I skim when I need a quick reminder. All of them shaped how I think about design.

- [Don't Make Me Think](https://sensible.com/dont-make-me-think/): The most accessible introduction to UX. Short, witty, and timeless.
- [The Design of Everyday Things](https://www.nngroup.com/books/design-everyday-things-revised/): The book that introduced "affordance" and "user experience" to the design vocabulary. Written in 1988, still relevant today.
- [100 Things Every Designer Needs to Know About People](https://www.amazon.com/Things-Designer-People-Voices-Matter/dp/0321767535): Psychology-backed design principles, each chapter 2-3 pages. Great for dipping in and out.
- [Creative Confidence](https://amzn.eu/d/068DNkNI): A powerful and inspiring book from the founders of IDEO, the award-winning design firm, on unleashing the creativity that lies within each and every one of us.
- [Buyology: Truth and Lies About Why We Buy](https://amzn.eu/d/06OCKW10): A fascinating look at how consumers perceive logos, ads, commercials, brands, and products.
- [Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion](https://amzn.eu/d/036FGHbV): Understanding and applying the six principles ethically is cost-free and deceptively easy.
- [Thinking, Fast and Slow](https://www.amazon.com/Thinking-Fast-Slow-Daniel-Kahneman/dp/0374533555): Nobel laureate's work on how humans think and decide. Heavy but worth it.
- [How to Get People to Do Stuff](https://www.amazon.com/People-Stuff-Master-Persuasion-Psychology/dp/0321884507): Motivation and behavioral triggers in design.
- [Mobile Design Pattern Gallery: UI Patterns for Smartphone Apps](https://a.co/d/0cCP1Fyy): A visual catalog of common mobile UI patterns. Navigation, forms, tables, search, onboarding, etc.

## 🎨 Design Principles & Psychology

The core principles we covered in the webinar. These don't change with trends or tools — they're rooted in how the human brain works.

- [Gestalt Principles](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/principles-visual-design/) — How the brain organizes visual information (Proximity, Similarity, Closure, Continuity, Figure/Ground).
- [Fitts's Law](https://lawsofux.com/fittss-law/) — Target size and distance determine interaction speed.
- [Hick's Law](https://lawsofux.com/hicks-law/) — More choices = slower decisions.
- [Miller's Law](https://lawsofux.com/millers-law/) — Working memory holds ~4 items. Use chunking.
- [Jakob's Law](https://lawsofux.com/jakobs-law/) — Users expect your site to work like the others they already know.
- [Peak-End Rule](https://lawsofux.com/peak-end-rule/) — Experiences are judged by their peak and final moments.
- [Zeigarnik Effect](https://lawsofux.com/zeigarnik-effect/) — Incomplete tasks are remembered better than completed ones.
- [Doherty Threshold](https://lawsofux.com/doherty-threshold/) — System response under 400ms feels instant.
- [Von Restorff Effect](https://lawsofux.com/von-restorff-effect/) — The item that stands out is the one remembered.
- [Tesler's Law](https://lawsofux.com/teslers-law/) — Every system has irreducible complexity. Push it behind the scenes.
- [Aesthetic-Usability Effect](https://lawsofux.com/aesthetic-usability-effect/) — Beautiful interfaces are perceived as easier to use.
- [NNGroup: Mental Models](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/mental-models/) — Understanding the gap between conceptual and mental models.
- [10 Usability Heuristics](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ten-usability-heuristics/) — Jakob Nielsen's 10 principles for interaction design. Industry standard since 1994.

## 🔧 Tools & Accessibility

Handy tools I use or recommend. Most of them are free, all of them are useful.

- [WebAIM Contrast Checker](https://webaim.org/resources/contrastchecker/) — Check color contrast ratios against WCAG AA/AAA standards.
- [Type Scale](https://typescale.com/) — Generate mathematically harmonious heading systems in seconds.
- [Coolors](https://coolors.co/) — Color palette generator.
- [Realtime Colors](https://www.realtimecolors.com/) — Preview your color palette on a real website layout.
- [Figma](https://www.figma.com/) — Collaborative interface design tool.
- [WCAG 2.1 Guidelines](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG21/) — Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. The official standard.
- [WebAIM](https://webaim.org/) — Web accessibility resources, tools, and training.
- [A11y Project](https://www.a11yproject.com/) — Community-driven accessibility checklist and resources.
- [Who Can Use](https://www.whocanuse.com/) — See how color contrast affects different types of vision.
- [Butterick's Practical Typography](https://practicaltypography.com/) — A comprehensive, opinionated guide to typography on the web.
- [Font Pair](https://www.fontpair.co/) — Curated typeface pairing suggestions.
